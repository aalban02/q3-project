Mathieu van der Poel is a man who has almost won everything within his powers. The 28-year-old has won Milan-San Remo, Paris-Roubaix, and the Tour of Flanders twice, stages of the Tour de France and the Giro d’Italia, over a glittering career.

The Dutchman has also been cyclo-cross world champion five times, and likes to dabble in mountain biking too.

Mathieu was born in Belgium to a French mother and a Dutch father, but races under the Dutch flag.

His grandfather on his mom's side was Raymond Poulidor, “the Eternal Second”, who finished second in the Tour de France three times, and third place five times. The Frenchman died in 2019
